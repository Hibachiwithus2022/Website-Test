Graduation season in Ontario is compressed, competitive, and held to a high standard. May and June are when three or four guests at your backyard can suddenly become forty — family from British Columbia, cousins from Quebec, aunts and uncles from across the GTA, the graduate's friend group filling the back row. You need a dinner format that scales, performs, and doesn't require you to be the one cooking.

A private hibachi chef for a graduation party solves all of it. One certified teppanyaki chef (or two, for larger groups), a propane teppan grill set up in your backyard or patio, 90 to 120 minutes of live cooking and entertainment — and your only job is standing next to your graduate when the first flame goes up.

This guide covers how to plan a graduation hibachi party in Ontario: when to book, how to set up for 25 to 60+ guests, what the event will look and feel like, and what makes the Ontario graduation market different from any US state we serve.

## Why Hibachi Works for Graduation Parties

Graduation parties have three requirements that most catering formats struggle to satisfy simultaneously: they need to feel special enough for the occasion, they need to scale comfortably to large mixed-age groups, and they need to be manageable for a host who already has enough to coordinate.

A private hibachi chef handles all three.

The live teppanyaki performance turns the backyard into a venue. Guests aren't just eating — they're watching fire tricks, flying shrimp, and a chef who runs the show for 90 minutes while cooking every plate to order. A 60-year-old uncle and a 20-year-old friend of the graduate are sitting within ten feet of each other, both laughing at the same moment. That multi-generational energy is extremely difficult to manufacture with drop-off catering or a restaurant reservation.

It also scales in a way that almost nothing else does. Ten guests at a dinner table feels different from 50 gathered around dual hibachi stations. The 50-person version is louder, more energetic, and more memorable — not harder to manage from the host's side, because the chef is running the room.

## Setup for Different Group Sizes

### Under 25 Guests — One Chef Station

A single chef station works comfortably for 10 to 25 guests. Everyone gathers around the single teppan grill in a tight semicircle. The performance is close-quarters and personal — the chef knows every guest's protein preferences, the show is directed at a unified crowd, and the whole event feels like an intimate dinner rather than a catered function. This is the right setup for smaller family celebrations, university graduation dinners, or graduation dinners for a close friend group.

### 25 to 50 Guests — Dual Chef Stations

For 25 to 50 guests, dual stations are strongly recommended. Two chefs working parallel grills cut per-person wait time in half, keep both groups actively engaged, and coordinate to produce a synchronized show that a single station can't replicate. The energy of two live grills running simultaneously is objectively more impressive than one.

In the [Mississauga](/locations/ontario/mississauga), [Brampton](/locations/ontario/brampton), and [Vaughan](/locations/ontario/vaughan) markets, dual-station bookings for June graduation parties are our standard setup, not our upgraded one.

### 50 to 80 Guests — Multiple Stations

Larger graduation parties (extended family reunions that happen to coincide with a graduation, or combined graduation and milestone celebration events) can be accommodated with additional stations. Contact us with your guest count and we'll recommend the right station configuration and timing plan.

## Backyard Setup Requirements

**Outdoor space:** At minimum 10×10 feet of flat, clear surface per chef station. A 20×20 area handles dual stations comfortably, with additional clearance for guests around both grills.

**Surface:** Wooden decks, stone patios, interlocking brick, and flat lawn all work. We bring non-slip mats to protect surfaces as needed.

**Seating:** You provide seating. Guests need to be able to sit close enough to see the grill — within about 8 to 12 feet is ideal. Patio furniture, rented folding tables and chairs, and lawn furniture all work. For 50+ guests, it's worth renting folding tables and chairs if you don't have enough on-site.

**Parking:** Not our concern, but for large groups in suburban neighbourhoods, give your guests a heads-up about street parking availability.

## What the Event Will Look Like

The chef arrives 30–45 minutes before your start time. Guests gather as setup completes. When the first flame goes up, the crowd's attention centralizes in a way that nothing else at a backyard party achieves quite so definitively.

The performance runs 90 to 120 minutes: fire tricks early (the volcano, the flaming grill show), then proteins cooked individually to order — chicken, steak, shrimp, salmon, tofu — two per person, prepared fresh as guests indicate their preference. Fried rice and grilled vegetables run throughout. Sauces are at the station for the duration.

The graduate is typically the first served, and the chef will acknowledge the occasion if you want them to. A brief moment where the chef directs the group's attention to the reason everyone is there — it's a small gesture that lands well.

When dinner is complete, the chef breaks down the station completely. Grill, propane, prep waste — all packed out. Your backyard is left clean.
