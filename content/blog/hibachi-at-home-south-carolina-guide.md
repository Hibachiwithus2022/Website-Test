South Carolina has six completely different private entertaining markets — and the occasion, the setting, and the reason someone books a private hibachi chef looks entirely different in each one.

The [Charleston](/locations/south-carolina/charleston) historic home dinner for a private anniversary at a South of Broad property is not the same occasion as the [Myrtle Beach](/locations/south-carolina/myrtle-beach) bachelorette weekend at the oceanfront rental. The [Greenville](/locations/south-carolina/greenville) corporate team dinner is not the same as the [Hilton Head Island](/locations/south-carolina/hilton-head-island) golf group reunion at a plantation villa. And the [Columbia](/locations/south-carolina/columbia) graduation party with sixty family members is its own market entirely.

This guide covers all six South Carolina markets: what makes each one distinctive, what occasions drive private hibachi demand there, and how to book in each.

---

## Charleston Historic Luxury: Charleston and Mount Pleasant

[Charleston](/locations/south-carolina/charleston) sets the standard for private entertaining in the Palmetto State. The South of Broad historic homes where a private teppanyaki chef on the garden terrace feels entirely appropriate to the address. The Daniel Island dinner parties that require a format capable of serving forty guests simultaneously. The Sullivan's Island and Isle of Palms vacation rental bachelorette weekends where the group wants the Saturday dinner to be the anchor of the trip rather than a reservation on King Street. The [Mount Pleasant](/locations/south-carolina/mount-pleasant) waterfront properties along the Cooper River where the graduation party or anniversary dinner needs to match the setting.

Charleston's private event market has a character found nowhere else in South Carolina — a combination of genuine historic identity, affluent professional and residential community, and destination tourism that produces a year-round demand for private dining experiences that no restaurant can replicate.

**What makes the Charleston market distinct:**
- Historic homes, garden terraces, and waterfront properties are the primary settings — the chef adapts to your space
- Sullivan's Island and Isle of Palms vacation rental bachelorette weekends are a major booking category
- Premium proteins (filet mignon, lobster tail, Chilean sea bass) are standard Charleston expectations
- Mount Pleasant's growing affluent residential community mirrors Charleston's standards

**When to book:** 3–4 weeks ahead for most events. Spring and fall are the most competitive periods. Bachelorette weekends on Sullivan's Island and Isle of Palms (May–September) book 4–6 weeks ahead.

## Upstate SC Corporate: Greenville, Spartanburg, and Simpsonville

[Greenville](/locations/south-carolina/greenville) is one of the fastest-growing cities in the Southeast, and the private event culture reflects its transformation. The Michelin, BMW, and GE corporate community that has established Greenville as a manufacturing and engineering hub of national significance. The Falls Park-area company dinners. The rooftop birthday celebration. The team dinner that the regional manager wants to be memorable enough to anchor the annual planning retreat.

[Spartanburg](/locations/south-carolina/spartanburg) and [Simpsonville](/locations/south-carolina/simpsonville) complete the Upstate SC market — Spartanburg's hub of manufacturing and university (Wofford, Converse) community, Simpsonville's growing Greenville-metro suburban residential culture. All three cities share a corporate and professional event profile that makes hibachi catering the preferred format when the company wants something more than a restaurant reservation.

**What makes Upstate SC distinct:**
- Corporate team dinners are a larger share of the market than in any other SC region
- BMW, Michelin, GE, and the Upstate manufacturing corridor generate consistent corporate demand
- Greenville's growth has created a strong residential market alongside the corporate one — graduation parties and milestone events are a significant secondary occasion
- Simpsonville's suburban growth mirrors the graduation culture of the Williamson County (TN) market — spring events book fast

**When to book:** 2–3 weeks ahead for most events. Corporate events and Greenville-area graduation season (May–June) should be booked 3 weeks ahead.

## Grand Strand Vacation: Myrtle Beach and North Myrtle Beach

[Myrtle Beach](/locations/south-carolina/myrtle-beach) and [North Myrtle Beach](/locations/south-carolina/north-myrtle-beach) are South Carolina's largest vacation destination market — and for private hibachi, they function exactly the way the Nashville and Gatlinburg vacation markets do: the group is at the rental, the restaurants on the Strand are crowded and not designed for large parties, and the solution is the chef coming to the beach house.

The Myrtle Beach bachelorette weekend that rented the oceanfront condo for fifteen people. The family reunion that found the house big enough for twenty-five. The corporate team that drove in from Charlotte for the long weekend and wants the pool deck dinner to be the kind of thing the team is still talking about at the next quarterly. Mobile hibachi in Myrtle Beach brings a certified chef to your vacation rental — deck, pool area, or backyard — with everything included and complete cleanup when dinner is done.

North Myrtle Beach has a slightly different character from Myrtle Beach proper — quieter, more family-oriented, the Cherry Grove and Crescent Beach communities that attract a different demographic than the Boardwalk corridor. The format is identical; the occasion type shifts slightly toward family reunions, anniversary trips, and milestone birthday weekends.

**What makes the Grand Strand distinct:**
- Vacation rental properties (beachfront condos, oceanfront houses, pool deck properties) are the primary setting
- Bachelorette groups of 10–20 are the most common Myrtle Beach booking format
- Family reunions and multi-family vacation groups of 25–40 require dual chef stations — note this at booking
- Peak summer season (June–August) and Memorial Day/Labor Day weekends book 4–6 weeks ahead

**When to book:** Peak summer: 4–6 weeks ahead. Shoulder season (April–May, September–October): 2–3 weeks. Off-season Grand Strand events have good availability with 1–2 weeks notice.

## Lowcountry Coastal: Hilton Head Island, Bluffton, and Pawleys Island

The Lowcountry coastal market covers three cities with distinct personalities but a shared outdoor luxury identity.

[Hilton Head Island](/locations/south-carolina/hilton-head-island) is a golf resort destination — Sea Pines, Palmetto Dunes, Shipyard, Port Royal. The golf group of twelve that rents the plantation villa for a week and wants the Saturday dinner at the house to be extraordinary. The corporate golf outing where dinner for twenty clients needs a format that matches the quality of the Harbour Town round they just played. Private hibachi catering at a Hilton Head plantation villa is one of the most premium private dining experiences we offer — premium protein packages, dual-station setup for larger groups, full cleanup.

[Bluffton](/locations/south-carolina/bluffton) is Hilton Head's growing residential counterpart — the communities along the Lowcountry marshes (Berkeley Hall, Colleton River, Hampton Hall) where the professional and retired community has built a genuinely affluent suburban identity with high private entertaining expectations. [Pawleys Island](/locations/south-carolina/pawleys-island) is a quieter, more residential coastal retreat north of Myrtle Beach — the historic beach house, the anniversary couple that chose the Pawleys Island cottage because the setting is extraordinary, the family that has been coming to the same beachfront house for twenty years and wants the Friday dinner to be the event of the week.

**What makes the Lowcountry distinct:**
- Hilton Head plantation villa settings are ideal for premium corporate and golf group entertaining
- Bluffton's gated golf community properties reflect residential standards as high as Charleston
- Pawleys Island occasions are typically milestone-specific — anniversaries, milestone birthdays, family traditions
- Travel fees may apply for Pawleys Island and remote Lowcountry properties — always disclosed upfront

**When to book:** Hilton Head peak season (spring and fall golf season, summer): 4–6 weeks ahead. Bluffton residential events: 2–3 weeks. Pawleys Island summer and holiday weekends: 3–4 weeks.

## Columbia Midlands: Columbia, Lexington, and Rock Hill

[Columbia](/locations/south-carolina/columbia) is South Carolina's capital and the home of the University of South Carolina — and the private event culture reflects both the government and professional community that has built Midlands SC, and the graduation season that marks the May calendar for families across the state.

USC graduation is the signature South Carolina event. The families who drove from Greenville, Charleston, and Charlotte to watch their student receive their diploma need the Saturday dinner to serve sixty people at a property that can hold the whole extended family. Columbia's residential community — the Trenholm Road corridor, Forest Acres, Northeast Columbia — also generates a strong year-round market for corporate events and milestone dinners tied to the state government and medical community. [Lexington](/locations/south-carolina/lexington) extends the Midlands market into Lexington County's growing suburban residential culture. [Rock Hill](/locations/south-carolina/rock-hill) anchors the York County market on the Charlotte metro border.

**What makes the Columbia Midlands distinct:**
- USC graduation (May) is the single largest hibachi booking event in the Columbia market — 40–80 guest parties with dual stations are common
- State government and healthcare professional events generate consistent year-round corporate demand
- Lexington County's residential growth means graduation season has expanded significantly
- Rock Hill's Charlotte metro proximity creates a cross-state market — Charlotte-area families with Rock Hill properties

**When to book:** USC graduation weekends: book in March or early April. Other Columbia events: 2–3 weeks ahead. Lexington and Rock Hill: 2 weeks ahead is generally sufficient.

## SC Suburban: Summerville and Greater Charleston

[Summerville](/locations/south-carolina/summerville) represents the newer residential growth around Charleston — the Dorchester County and Berkeley County suburban communities that have expanded dramatically as Charleston has grown. The graduation parties happening every May in the new neighborhoods along Trolley Road and the Sawmill Branch corridor. The young professional family communities that have built out across the Ashley River corridor. The backyard parties that are exactly what private hibachi was designed to serve.

Summerville's growth has made it one of the fastest-growing communities in South Carolina — and the event profile follows. More graduation parties each spring, more milestone events tied to the growing professional community, more backyard hibachi events in the subdivisions that have emerged over the past decade.

**What makes the SC Suburban market distinct:**
- Backyard graduation parties are the dominant occasion — May and June are the peak window
- Groups of 20–40 are common — dual stations for larger events
- Proximity to Charleston means some events serve guests who live in both markets
- Year-round availability outside graduation season; summers are busy but not at the vacation rental intensity of the Strand

**When to book:** 2–3 weeks ahead for most events. May and June graduation Saturdays: 3–4 weeks ahead.

## What Every South Carolina Private Hibachi Event Includes

Regardless of which SC market you're in — Charleston terrace, Myrtle Beach vacation rental, Hilton Head plantation villa, Greenville corporate venue, Columbia backyard, or Summerville subdivision — here's what every event includes:

**Your chef brings:**
- Propane teppan grill (self-contained — no gas hookups required at any property)
- All proteins cooked to order — standard menu plus premium upgrades (filet mignon, lobster tail, Chilean sea bass, wagyu, king shrimp)
- Fried rice, vegetables, miso soup, garden salad
- House-made sauces
- Plates, chopsticks, serving utensils

**What you provide:**
- A flat outdoor area of at least 10×10 feet (deck, patio, backyard, pool area, garden terrace)
- Seating for your guests
- For vacation rental and remote coastal properties: vehicle access and any gate codes provided 24 hours ahead

**The format:**
- Chef arrives 20–30 minutes before start for setup
- Performance runs 90–120 minutes from first flame to final plate
- Fire tricks, flying shrimp, precision knife work — the full live teppanyaki show
- Complete cleanup when dinner is done

**Pricing:** Starting at $60 per adult, $30 per child (ages 4–12), $600 event minimum. Premium proteins available as upgrades per guest. Groups over 25: dual chef stations recommended.
