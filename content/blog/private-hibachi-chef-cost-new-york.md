The first question every potential New York host asks is what it actually costs. The honest answer is slightly more nuanced than a single number — because New York is more nuanced than a single market. A corporate dinner in [New York City](/locations/new-york/new-york-city) involves different logistics than a [Scarsdale](/locations/new-york/scarsdale) graduation party, which is different from a [Lake George](/locations/new-york/lake-george) Adirondack lake house reunion or an [East Hampton](/locations/new-york/east-hampton) summer house dinner for twenty-four.

Here's a complete breakdown of what private hibachi actually costs in New York in 2025 — the base rate, the factors that move it, how the math works across different group sizes, and how it stacks up against restaurant hibachi in the city.

## The Base Rate

The starting point is the same across all New York markets:

- **Adults (13+): $60 per person**
- **Children ages 4–12: $30 per child**
- **Children 3 and under: Free**
- **Event minimum: $600**

That $600 minimum means you need either 10 adults or a combination that totals at least that amount. Most New York events run well above the minimum — average party sizes across the state fall between 15 and 25 guests, putting most events between $900 and $1,500 before add-ons.

## What the Price Covers

This is not a food delivery situation. The base rate includes everything you'd expect from a full hibachi restaurant experience — except it happens at your home or rental property:

- A certified, experienced teppanyaki chef
- The full propane teppan grill and all cooking equipment
- All proteins and ingredients (your two included selections per person)
- All vegetables, fried rice, and house-made sauces
- Complete setup before the event (20–30 minutes)
- 90–120 minutes of live hibachi cooking and entertainment
- Full teardown and cleanup when dinner is done

You provide: outdoor space, tables, chairs, and whatever you're serving to drink. Nothing else.

## Travel Fees in New York

New York is a large state. Travel fees depend on the distance from the nearest available chef's location.

**No travel fee:** Most of the NYC metro area — the five boroughs, Long Island through the South Fork, Westchester County, and the immediate Connecticut and New Jersey border markets. Standard service zones with no additional charge.

**Travel fee may apply:** Locations more than 35–40 miles from the nearest chef base. In New York, this most commonly affects:

- **The Hamptons** — particularly [East Hampton](/locations/new-york/east-hampton), Montauk, and properties east of the Shinnecock Canal. The further east, the more likely a travel fee applies.
- **Upstate locations** — [Lake George](/locations/new-york/lake-george), the Adirondack region, the Catskills, and remote Hudson Valley properties.
- **[Saratoga Springs](/locations/new-york/saratoga-springs)** — typically within the travel fee threshold depending on chef availability.

Travel fees are always disclosed before you confirm your booking. There are no surprise charges after the fact. When you inquire, ask specifically about travel fees for your location if you're outside the metro area.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is $600 really the minimum even for a small group?**
Yes. A group of 8 adults totals $480 at base rate — the $600 event minimum applies. The value per person improves as group size increases; the sweet spot starts at 12–15 guests.

**Can guests split the cost using Venmo or Zelle?**
Absolutely — and many hosts do exactly this. The host books and receives payment from the group. $60–$70 per person collected in advance is a common approach.

**What's included in "full cleanup"?**
Everything. The chef dismantles all equipment, packages all waste, and removes it from your property. Your outdoor space looks the same way it did before they arrived.

**Is the per-person rate the same for Hamptons and Upstate events?**
Yes — $60/adult is consistent. Travel fees are separate and location-dependent. Always confirm during your quote process.

**Do you offer any group discounts for large New York events?**
Our pricing is consistent per-person. For very large events (60+ guests), contact us directly for a custom quote — multi-station configurations and extended service times can be arranged.

**How does the event minimum work for mixed adult/child groups?**
Children 4–12 are $30. A party of 8 adults and 4 children totals $600 ($480 adults + $120 children), which meets the minimum exactly. Any combination totaling $600 or more works.
