Missouri doesn't have one outdoor entertaining culture. It has several, and they're genuinely distinct. [Kansas City](/locations/missouri/kansas-city) built its identity around the backyard — the cedar deck, the Weber kettle, the gathering that runs from noon until midnight with the whole neighborhood welcome. [St. Louis](/locations/missouri/st-louis) has the brick backyard and the south city porch, the [Chesterfield](/locations/missouri/chesterfield) estate terrace, the [Kirkwood](/locations/missouri/kirkwood) garden dinner. [Columbia](/locations/missouri/columbia) has Mizzou graduation season, where every backyard near campus becomes a graduation venue for three weekends straight in May. And [Lake of the Ozarks](/locations/missouri/lake-of-the-ozarks) has the lake house — the pontoon dock, the cove, the group house rental where one great dinner anchors the entire weekend.

Private hibachi at home fits every one of these markets. A certified teppanyaki chef arrives at your outdoor space with a propane teppan grill, all fresh proteins and vegetables, house-made sauces, plates, chopsticks, and everything needed to run a 90–120 minute live cooking performance in front of your guests. You provide the outdoor space and the chairs. The chef handles everything else — including complete cleanup when dinner is done.

This guide covers every major Missouri market: what makes each one distinct, how to book correctly for your situation, and what to expect from start to finish.

## What's Included in a Missouri Hibachi at Home Event

No guest needs to go anywhere. The chef comes to your property.

**What the chef brings:**
- Propane teppan grill (fully self-contained)
- All fresh proteins and vegetables
- House-made sauces (ginger, yum yum, teriyaki)
- Fried rice ingredients
- Miso soup and garden salad
- Plates, chopsticks, serving utensils
- Complete setup and teardown

**What you provide:**
- A flat, stable outdoor area — at least 10×10 feet
- Tables and chairs for your guests
- Access to your outdoor space

That's the entire list. Everything else is handled.

**The performance:** Once guests are gathered around the grill, the chef runs 90–120 minutes of live teppanyaki — the onion volcano, fire tricks, flying shrimp, egg tosses, and every protein and vegetable cooked to order in front of your group. Every guest gets exactly what they ordered, fresh at the grill.

## Pricing for Missouri Events

The standard rate is **$60 per adult, $30 per child (ages 4–12)**. Children under 3 eat free. There is a $600 event minimum.

That covers the chef, the full performance, all ingredients, complete setup, and cleanup. No venue fee. No service charge. No drink markup.

Premium protein upgrades are available for any Missouri event: filet mignon, lobster tail, and additional protein selections. These are popular for Chesterfield estate dinners, Kirkwood milestone celebrations, and St. Louis anniversary events where the occasion calls for it.

For large groups (25+), we bring two chef stations running simultaneously. Missouri graduation parties and family reunions of 30–60+ guests are common — dual station is standard for these sizes.

## Missouri's Six Major Markets

### Kansas City Metro — Backyard Capital of Missouri

[Kansas City](/locations/missouri/kansas-city) is built around the backyard. The outdoor entertaining culture here is more developed than anywhere else in Missouri — the deck built specifically for groups, the tailgate tradition that extends well beyond Arrowhead into every neighborhood in the metro, the neighbors who know your kids' names because the gate is genuinely always open. A private backyard hibachi party in Kansas City brings a certified teppanyaki chef to that setup with a completely different fire performance than the usual cookout.

The KC metro is one of our most active Missouri markets. Graduation season (May–June) is the most competitive booking window — reserve 3–4 weeks ahead for any Saturday event in the metro.

**[Lee's Summit](/locations/missouri/lee-s-summit)** is among the most popular suburban markets in the metro. The Lakewood and Legacy Park neighborhoods have exactly the outdoor setups private hibachi was designed for — large, flat, accessible backyards built for groups. Lee's Summit hosts are organized and expect professional service from first inquiry to final cleanup. Graduation parties, anniversary dinners, and milestone birthday celebrations are the most common bookings.

**[Blue Springs](/locations/missouri/blue-springs)** anchors the eastern KC metro. The backyard culture is strong here, and guest counts tend to run higher than the metro average — graduation parties of 30–50+ guests are common during summer. The Adams Dairy and Woods Chapel Road corridors have produced some of our most enthusiastic KC crowds.

**What to know for the KC metro:** Summer weekends and graduation season (May–June) book 3–4 weeks out. Chiefs season and holiday party windows also move fast. We serve the full KC metro on both sides of the Missouri–Kansas border — Lee's Summit, Blue Springs, Independence, Raytown, Overland Park, Lenexa, Leawood, and beyond. Most locations have no extra travel fee.

### St. Louis Metro — Private Dining Tradition

[St. Louis](/locations/missouri/st-louis) has a different outdoor entertaining culture than Kansas City — more private, more neighborhood-specific, and with a long tradition of home entertaining that runs from south city brick backyards to west county residential estates. Private hibachi fits the city's hosting culture naturally.

Graduation season in the St. Louis metro is intense. With Lafayette, Chaminade, MICDS, and dozens of high schools running graduation weekends in May and early June — and with Washington University and SLU adding to the calendar — spring booking windows fill fast. Our most common St. Louis booking is a graduation party of 20–50 guests hosted in a residential backyard where every restaurant in the metro is already at capacity.

**[O'Fallon](/locations/missouri/o-fallon)** is St. Charles County's largest city and one of the fastest-growing communities in Missouri. The young family demographic here books heavily during graduation season and birthday party season. O'Fallon's residential neighborhoods — particularly along Highway 40 — have spacious outdoor setups that handle 20–40-guest events with room to spare.

**[St. Charles](/locations/missouri/st-charles)** brings historical character and strong family culture to the northwest corner of the STL metro. The neighborhoods near the Missouri River and the downtown Main Street corridor see consistent hibachi demand from community-minded households who treat a graduation party or anniversary dinner as a proper occasion worth doing right.

**What to know for the STL metro:** Weekend events in St. Louis and St. Charles County typically book 2–3 weeks out. Graduation season (May–June) is our most competitive window — reserve 4–5 weeks ahead for any Saturday graduation event anywhere in the metro.

### St. Louis County — Estate Dining and Affluent Suburbs

[Chesterfield](/locations/missouri/chesterfield) sets the private entertaining standard for St. Louis County. The estate homes along Clarkson Road, Long Road, and throughout the Chesterfield Valley were designed for exactly this kind of evening — the stone terrace, the wide rear yard, the property that was professionally landscaped for a reason. Private hibachi in Chesterfield brings a certified teppanyaki chef to your property with hand-selected premium proteins and a performance that earns its place at a Chesterfield outdoor table.

**[Kirkwood](/locations/missouri/kirkwood)** is the St. Louis suburb that set the template. Victorian homes and craftsman bungalows, established backyards and garden spaces in Woodbine and along Argonne Drive, a resident culture that has been hosting proper dinner parties for generations. Kirkwood graduation season and milestone anniversary dinners are among the most elegantly organized events we run in Missouri.

**What to know for St. Louis County:** Events in Chesterfield and Kirkwood book 3–4 weeks ahead. Graduation season fills fastest. Premium protein packages — filet mignon, lobster tail — are available for estate events and milestone occasions.

[Columbia](/locations/missouri/columbia) in May is graduation season at its most concentrated. University of Missouri commencement fills every hotel, restaurant, and Airbnb within 30 miles of campus. A private hibachi chef at your Columbia home or backyard eliminates that problem entirely — the chef comes to you, performs for 90–120 minutes, and every guest gets exactly what they ordered without anyone leaving the property.

**What to know for Mid-Missouri:** Columbia graduation season (May–June) books 4–5 weeks ahead. Springfield graduation season books 3–4 weeks out. Off-season dates typically have 2–3 weeks of flexibility.

### Southwest Missouri — Joplin and the Four-State Region

[Joplin](/locations/missouri/joplin) anchors Southwest Missouri with genuine community spirit — birthday parties that bring three generations to the same backyard, graduation celebrations with out-of-town family arriving from Kansas City and Springfield, family reunions where the host takes pride in the occasion. Hibachi catering in Joplin brings a certified teppanyaki chef to your property for all of these events with the full setup and a live-fire performance that makes any Joplin gathering the dinner guests are still talking about next month.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What do I need to provide at my Missouri home?**
Tables and chairs for your guests. The chef brings everything else: grill, propane, all proteins, vegetables, fried rice, sauces, plates, chopsticks, and handles complete setup and full cleanup.

**Can hibachi be set up in a Missouri backyard, deck, or patio?**
Yes — residential backyards, patios, decks, and outdoor spaces are the most common setups throughout Missouri. You need a flat area of at least 10×10 feet with overhead clearance. Most Missouri residential properties work perfectly.

**How many guests can you handle?**
One chef station serves up to 20–25 guests. For groups over 25, we bring two chef stations running simultaneously. Missouri graduation parties and family reunions of 30–60+ guests are common — dual station is standard for these sizes.

**Does the KC metro service cover both sides of the state line?**
Yes — we serve the full KC metro on both sides of the Missouri–Kansas border: Lee's Summit, Blue Springs, Independence, Raytown, Overland Park, Lenexa, Leawood, Prairie Village, Olathe, and beyond. Most KC metro locations fall within our standard service area with no extra travel fee.

**What about Missouri spring weather?**
Light rain under adequate shelter (covered deck, high pergola) is workable. For severe weather or lightning, we reschedule at no penalty. Mention your backup shelter situation when you inquire.

**Is hibachi good for a Mizzou graduation party in Columbia?**
It's the best format for it. When every restaurant near campus is fully booked on graduation Saturday, a private hibachi chef eliminates the logistics problem entirely. The chef performs at your home while you host — 90–120 minutes of live entertainment for your entire group, every dish cooked to order.

**Can I add premium proteins for a Chesterfield or Kirkwood estate event?**
Yes — filet mignon, lobster tail, and additional protein selections are available as upgrades for any Missouri event. St. Louis County estate events frequently use premium upgrades for milestone and anniversary occasions.
