There is a certain tier of Northern Virginia entertaining where a restaurant reservation is not the right answer.

It isn't a question of quality. The Tysons corridor has excellent restaurants. Old Town Alexandria has exceptional ones. Downtown DC is thirty minutes away. The question is what a restaurant fundamentally cannot do: give a group of twenty people an exclusive, private, unhurried evening that belongs entirely to them, at a property that reflects who they are and what they've built.

[McLean](/locations/virginia/mclean) estate hosts, [Leesburg](/locations/virginia/leesburg) wine country property owners, Great Falls and Vienna families who entertain at a high standard, and [Charlottesville](/locations/virginia/charlottesville) vineyard-adjacent property owners have all discovered the same thing in recent years: a certified private hibachi chef at your outdoor space produces the evening that your guests are still talking about at the next occasion. Not the Tysons restaurant they've been to three dozen times.

This is why private hibachi for estate entertaining in Northern Virginia has become what it is.

## What Makes Estate Hibachi Different

The private hibachi format scales to the expectations of Northern Virginia's most discerning hosts in ways that standard formats don't.

**Exclusivity:** The chef performs exclusively for your group. There are no adjacent tables, no shared dining rooms, no strangers. The event happens at your property, in your outdoor space, for your guests — and no one else.

**Premium proteins:** The standard menu is excellent. The premium menu is exceptional. Filet mignon, A5 wagyu beef, lobster tail, Chilean sea bass, jumbo scallops, and king shrimp are all available as individual guest upgrades — so one guest can have the filet and lobster while another has salmon and shrimp, all cooked fresh at the teppan. McLean estate events most commonly include filet mignon and lobster tail as standard upgrades for the group. Charlottesville vineyard dinners often add Chilean sea bass to complement the evening's wine selections.

**Scale:** One chef station serves up to 20–25 guests comfortably. For estate gatherings of 25 or more, we deploy two chef stations running simultaneously — coordinated, orchestrated, but operating as a single show. Large anniversary parties, milestone birthday celebrations, and political and diplomatic entertaining events of 40–80 guests are among our most common Northern Virginia large-format bookings.

**Complete discretion:** The chef arrives, performs, and departs with everything they brought. Your property is exactly as it was before they arrived. For high-discretion events — embassy entertaining, executive team dinners, private fundraisers — this is a non-negotiable feature that no restaurant can offer.

## What the Evening Actually Looks Like

At most Northern Virginia estate events, the flow runs like this:

**Before the chef arrives:** Your guests are gathered on the terrace or estate grounds. You've chosen proteins for the group — or allowed individual selections at booking — and we have everything confirmed.

**Chef arrival (60–90 minutes before service):** The chef arrives, unloads the full setup, and has the teppan grill positioned and the mise en place arranged before any guests approach the station. Setup is thorough and efficient. Premium estate events get additional mise en place time for the full premium menu.

**The performance (90–120 minutes):** Guests gather around the grill. The chef opens with the salad course and miso soup, then moves through the full teppanyaki performance — fire tricks, the onion volcano, knife work, egg tosses, flying shrimp — while cooking every protein and vegetable to order at the grill. Premium proteins are cooked with additional precision and plated individually.

**After the final course:** The chef handles complete teardown and cleanup. Your estate terrace, garden, or patio is exactly as it was before the chef arrived. No dishes, no cleanup, nothing left behind.

The total footprint of the evening is the evening. Your guests leave; your property is immaculate.

**How far in advance should I book for a major estate event?**
