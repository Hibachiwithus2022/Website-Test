## What Makes Charleston Different

Charleston has three distinct private dining markets layered on top of each other — and private hibachi serves all three well.

**The historic residential market.** The families and professionals who live in the South of Broad neighborhood, in the Harleston Village and Radcliffeborough historic districts, in the Wagener Terrace and Hampton Park communities north of the Crosstown — these are the Charleston residents who mark milestones at home because home is already extraordinary. The private hibachi chef at the garden terrace, the piazza dinner for twenty, the anniversary celebration at the East Battery house. Charleston's residential dining culture is private by nature.

**The vacation rental and bachelorette market.** Sullivan's Island and Isle of Palms are Charleston's bachelorette coastal market. The oceanfront vacation rental on Sullivan's Island that the bride picked because the whole group can stay there, the Isle of Palms beach house with the deck that faces the Atlantic. These groups have flown in from across the Southeast and the East Coast. They want dinner at the house, not a King Street restaurant with a two-hour wait on a Saturday in May. The chef comes to the rental. Performs for the group. Leaves the property immaculate.

**The Mount Pleasant residential market.** [Mount Pleasant](/locations/south-carolina/mount-pleasant) has grown into one of the most affluent residential communities in South Carolina — the Dunes West and Rivertowne families, the I'On and Old Village neighborhoods, the waterfront properties along the Wando River and the Shem Creek corridor. Mount Pleasant graduation parties routinely reach 40–60 guests. The outdoor space at a Mount Pleasant home is intentional entertaining space. Private hibachi fits exactly.

## The Sullivan's Island Bachelorette Weekend Format

The Sullivan's Island bachelorette weekend has become one of the signature Charleston hibachi bookings — and the format is worth understanding for anyone planning one.

The typical Sullivan's Island bachelorette hibachi event:

**Group size:** 12–20 guests is most common. For groups over 25, we bring two chefs running simultaneously so nobody waits for their plate.

**Setting:** Ocean-facing deck or backyard of the vacation rental. The chef arrives fully self-contained — no gas hookup at the rental is needed. Setup takes 20 minutes.

**Timing:** Most bachelorette groups book the hibachi dinner as the evening anchor before going out — starting at 5:30 or 6:00 PM. The performance runs 90–120 minutes from first flame to final plate.

**Proteins:** The bachelorette upgrade packages are popular — filet mignon, lobster tail, and premium king shrimp alongside the standard chicken and steak menu. These are worth considering if the group wants the elevated experience.

**The cleanup question:** This comes up every time. When dinner is done, the chef breaks down the setup completely and takes everything out. The vacation rental's deck and outdoor space are left exactly as they were. No grease, no mess, no additional cleanup for the group.

**When to book:** Sullivan's Island and Isle of Palms summer weekends (May–September) book 4–6 weeks ahead. The most popular bachelorette weekends — Memorial Day, Fourth of July, Labor Day — book as early as two months out. Don't wait.

## Mount Pleasant Graduation and Milestone Events

[Mount Pleasant](/locations/south-carolina/mount-pleasant) graduation parties have their own specific profile — and it's worth planning for if you're organizing one.

The typical Mount Pleasant graduation event is larger than you expect. The extended family who drove from Columbia and Charlotte. The high school friends from Wando and Lucy Beckham. The church community. The neighborhood that has watched this kid grow up. By the time the guest list is finalized, it's forty people. Sometimes sixty.

For groups over 25, we bring two certified chefs and two teppan stations operating simultaneously. Both chefs perform at the same time — the cooking is synchronized, the timing matches, and the full group eats within the same 90-minute window rather than waiting for one chef to rotate through the whole guest list. This is the dual-station format, and it's how graduation events in Mount Pleasant work.

**What to note for Mount Pleasant graduation events:**
- Confirm guest count at booking — dual stations are arranged in advance and can't be added last-minute
- Outdoor space requirements: two stations need approximately 20×10 feet of clearance
- Graduation Saturday (mid-May through early June) books 3–4 weeks ahead — plan accordingly
- Premium protein packages are available but should be confirmed with the full group ahead of time
